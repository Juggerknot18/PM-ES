Research

Engineering questions before product claims

PM-ES is organised around engineering questions rather than product claims. Public research content distinguishes design intent, software-level simulation and measured hardware results.

Workstreams

Permanent-magnet machine prototyping

Exploration of custom machine geometries, position feedback and electromechanical integration. Public content focuses on architecture and methodology; measured characteristics will be published only after controlled testing.

Embedded motor supervision

A dedicated STM32 supervisor coordinates states, prerequisites, command ramps, diagnostics and event records. It does not replace low-level FOC and does not override autonomous bus protection.

DC-bus monitoring and protection

The project studies how returned energy is observed, limited and handled by a safety-oriented architecture without turning software into the single authority for protection.

Regenerative energy management

Regenerative strategies are investigated as system-level questions. Public material does not claim a final energy-recovery solution.

Diagnostics and observability

Monitoring and event data support interpretation, staged testing and future reports without exposing private protocols or implementation details.

Staged experimental validation

Validation progresses through software-level simulation, interface bring-up, low-energy bench testing, subsystem verification and integrated tests.
