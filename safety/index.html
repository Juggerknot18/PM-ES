Safety

Hardware protection remains authoritative

Hardware protection remains authoritative. Software adds supervision, diagnostics and controlled behaviour without becoming a single point of failure for DC-bus protection.

Safety-oriented principles

  • Critical protection must remain active through MCU reset or failure.
  • Supervisory tasks are designed around non-blocking behaviour.
  • Low-level FOC remains outside the STM32 supervisor.
  • Normal operation requires valid prerequisites and explicit state coordination.
  • Monitoring interfaces do not gain active control authority by default.
  • Faults escalate by reducing or stopping the cause, not by suppressing protection.
Experimental project notice. PM-ES is an experimental engineering project. Public documentation describes design intent and development status; it is not a certification, a safety manual or an instruction to reproduce high-energy hardware without qualified review.
